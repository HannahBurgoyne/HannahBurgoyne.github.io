Foundations Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long-term plan beyond Dev Academy is to enter the tech industry as a web developer. I really enjoy working on projects for others and helping them to realise their vision - it's something I did in a different context during my previous line of work before coming to Dev Academy, when I was working in an art gallery. As part of a team I set up and installed audiovisual installations to meet respective artists' specifications, based on their vision for how their artwork would look installed in a gallery setting. With the skills I gain from Foundations and Bootcamp, I would love to work with a team of fellow developers to realise clients' visions for their websites, and contribute to building and maintaining the web.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I learn really well by trying something out and tinkering with it. Before enrolling here I was tasked at my former workplace to set up a new website using Wordpress and the Divi theme. I had never build a website before, but I began learning how to use the CMS and the basics of building a site by experimenting with different strategies and designs, doing a lot of online research and having many conversations with my manager on what her vision was for the website and its content. Ultimately I found that trying out a piece of code really helped me understand how it worked. I continued with this learning strategy when I did some further interactive courses through FreeCodeCamp and The Odin Project, picking up further CSS skills and diving into JavaScript for the first time. I also find visual content really helpful in supporting my learning process, which I think is part of why website development appeals to me - being able to test something out in Dev Tools and seeing it update visually in the browser window helps me understand what my code is actually doing.

When something isn't working how I expect, it can be a challenge for me to not clam up and try to problem solve it entirely on my own instead of asking for help. I have a tendency to get lost in what I'm doing and become entirely fixated on it, to the extent that I can forget to take breaks or be unable to mentally step away from the issue and give my brain a rest. In the past I've always found myself coding and problem solving alone, and had to be entirely reliant on myself and the internet. I'm keen to work on reaching out for support and asking others for their advice on my code. I'd also like to work more on not comparing my own learning journey to others, and to be inspired by others' creations rather than comparing theirs to my own.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

One of my main goals at Dev Academy is to learn how to program and problem solve with others as part of a team. I've worked in teams before but never in a tech-oriented environment, so I believe this will be a very valuable aspect of my learning journey and it's a big part of why I enroled in the first place. I know from my past work experience that teamwork is extremely important in any profession, and by learning to code at home on my own I was missing out on that aspect of retraining as a developer. I've never really gotten feedback on my code before, so this is a great opportunity to learn to recieve and give feedback and to put it to use constructively.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme

I am treating my time at Dev Academy as my full-time job. This means that I have a Monday - Friday 9am - 5pm routine during which I will be focused and ready to work, and will write myself a list of daily goals and evaluate this at the end of each day. I will take my work seriously, and put my best effort into everything I do. I will track my time and be focused on the course content, and engage with my peers. I will take breaks when I need them and practice self-care to sustain myself through each challenge which comes my way. If I am unable to meet a deadline, I will speak to my Facilitator ahead of time and discuss what can be done. I will always engage with my peers, Facilitators and industry/community representatives in a professional and warm manner, as I would do in my personal and professional life.

When things are getting challenging and overwhelming, I will take a productive and constructive approach. I will take a break, recognise when I am out of my depth, and reach out to my Facilitator(s) for guidance and discuss the best path forward. I will be ready and willing to make any suggested changes to my schedule and/or time management to help ease the pressure.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I will seek help when I am unable to resolve a problem on my own, and will reach out in a timely fashion to my peers and Facilitators on Discord (during Foundations) or in person (during Bootcamp). I will describe my issue as best as I can, ask clarifying questions and try out suggested ideas to see if they work. I am grateful for any help I receive, and will try to give help as often as I can in return.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect the Facilitators to continue to keep up the great work in being supportive, friendly, encouraging and actively engaging with myself and my peers each week to check in with us and help us where needed.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things

I am fortunate to have no regular commitments at present outside of Dev Academy. I am single, flatting, and have no dependents. However, I will be mindful of any upcoming commitments such as appointments and social activities, and will plan around these. Where possible I will try to schedule these outside of 9am - 5pm weekdays, but when this is not possible I will ensure it doesn't intefere with my ability to complete my work and I will comunicate any clashes with the Foundations/Bootcamp schedule as far ahead of time as possible to my Facilitators.
