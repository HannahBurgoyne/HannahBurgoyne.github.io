Te Whare Tapa Whā

Caring for my whole self

03 July, 2023

Introduction

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori perspective on health, developed by Tā Mason Durie. The model provides four dimensions of wellbeing which need to be nourished:

  • taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • taha whānau (family wellbeing)

All four of these concepts connect to the whenua upon which we stand. Te Whare Tapa Whā can be thought of as a wharenui, sitting upon the land and acting as the foundation to our wellbeing.

Below, I will share how I plan to incorporate Te Whare Tapa Whā into my learning journey at Dev Academy. This will be my personal wellbeing plan going forward.

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

  • Regular exercise everyday - getting my body moving in some way
  • Time away from the screen through regular breaks
  • Practising good sleep hygiene, getting enough rest
  • Eating three meals a day, including nutritious food

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

  • Keeping in regular contact with my family, sharing my journey
  • Making time to connect with friends and loved ones each week
  • Hanging out with my flatmates in the evenings after finishing study
  • Socialising with other members of my cohort, being active in the Discord servers

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

  • Keeping up a regular mindfulness practice
  • Giving myself breaks when I'm stuck/blocked mentally
  • Encouraging and praising myself as I learn, being gentle to myself
  • Reaching out for help and support when I need it

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

  • Daily meditation to introspect and check in with myself
  • Believing in myself while I move through challenges
  • Being grateful for what's going well, focusing on what I've achieved so far
  • Finding moments throughout the day to appreciate life

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

  • Joining my flatmate on regular dog walks in nature
  • Keeping my surroundings tidy and organised
  • Spending time sitting outdoors away from the screen each day
  • Looking after the plants surrounding my workspace
